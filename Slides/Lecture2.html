Isomorphisms

When are two graphs

"the same"?

An isomorphism of graphs is a bijection between the vertices that preserves the number of edges.

More precisely, an isomorphism between graphs $G$ and $H$ is a bijection $\varphi:V(G)\to V(H)$ so that for any $v,w\in V(G)$, the number of edges between $v$ and $w$ (in $G$) is the same as the number of edges between $\varphi(v)$ and $\varphi(w)$ (in $H$).
